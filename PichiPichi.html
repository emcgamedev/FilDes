Pichi-Pichi

Steamed cassava treats with a chewy texture and vibrant colors, often filled with cheese or ube halaya (purple yam jam).

prep time

20 min.

cook time

0 min.

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1

Prepare the lye water solution: Wear gloves for safety. In a small bowl, dilute 1 tbsp lye water with 1/4 cup cool water. Set aside. Note: Be very careful when handling lye water, and avoid contact with skin or eyes.

step 2

Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together cassava flour and sugar.

step 3

Gradually add liquids: Slowly add water while whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Add the diluted lye water solution and continue whisking until well combined.

step 4

Divide and color (optional): If using multiple colors, divide the batter into portions. Add a few drops of your desired food coloring to each portion and mix well.

step 5

Prepare the filling (optional): If using cheese or ube halaya, cut them into small cubes.

step 6

Steaming: Grease banana leaves and line the bottom of your steamer. Pinch a small amount of batter and flatten it slightly. Place a piece of cheese or ube halaya filling in the center (if using). Fold the dough to enclose the filling and shape it into a ball. Arrange the formed pichi-pichi on the banana leaves. Steam for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.

step 7

Cooling and serving: Let the pichi-pichi cool completely before removing them from the banana leaves. Serve chilled.

Ingredients

Cassava flour (2 cups)

Water (3 cups)

Sugar (1/2 cup)

Lye water (1 tbsp, diluted solution) - handle with care

Food coloring (desired color)

Cheese cubes (optional, for filling)

Ube halaya (optional, for filling)

Banana leaves (for lining the steamer)

Tools

Large Bowl

steamer

whisk

spoon

banana leaves

gloves (for handling lye water)
