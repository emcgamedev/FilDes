Kutsinta

Steamed rice cakes made with cassava flour and brown sugar, known for their wobbly texture and vibrant colors.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

0 min.

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1

Prepare the lye water solution: Wear gloves for safety. In a small bowl, dilute 1 tbsp lye water with 1/4 cup cool water. Set aside. Note: Be very careful when handling lye water, and avoid contact with skin or eyes.

step 2

Mix the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together cassava flour and brown sugar.

step 3

Gradually add liquids: Slowly add water while whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Add the diluted lye water solution and continue whisking until well combined.

step 4

Divide and color: Divide the batter into portions if using multiple food colors. Add a few drops of your desired food coloring to each portion and mix well.

step 5

Steaming: Grease banana leaves and line the bottom of your steamer. Pour the batter into molds or small ramekins lined with banana leaves. Steam for 30-40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.

step 6

Cooling and serving: Let the kutsinta cool completely before removing them from the molds. Serve chilled.

Ingredients

Cassava flour (2 cups)

Water (4 cups)

Brown sugar (1 cup)

Lye water (1 tbsp, diluted solution) - handle with care

Food coloring (desired color)

Banana leaves (for lining the steamer)

Tools

Large Bowl

whisk

Spoon

measuring cups

steamer

molds or ramekins

banana leaves (optional, for lining)

gloves (for handling lye water)
