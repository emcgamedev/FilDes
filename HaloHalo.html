Halo-Halo

Halo-halo is a shaved ice dessert which is considered the unofficial national dessert of the Philippines. Its name translates to "mix-mix" in Tagalog, which perfectly describes the delightful experience of enjoying this sweet treat.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

0 min.

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1

Prepare your ingredients

step 2

In a tall glass, layer in a spoonful or two of each of your chosen ingredients. You can start with the beans, then jellies, fruits, and pinipig.

step 3

Pack the glass with shaved ice, pressing down gently to make space for everything.

step 4

Drizzle with evaporated milk or coconut milk until it reaches your desired level.

step 5

Top it off with a dollop of ube jam and a slice of leche flan (if using).

step 6

Mix and enjoy!

ingredients

Shaved ice (crushed ice works too in a pinch)

Evaporated milk or coconut milk

Sweetened beans (mung beans, chickpeas, etc.) - pre-made or cooked from scratch

Candied fruits and jellies (jackfruit, saba bananas, gulaman, nata de coco) - canned or homemade

Pinipig (pounded young rice)

Ube jam (purple yam jam)

Leche flan (optional)

Ube ice cream (or your favorite flavor)

tools

Ice Shaver or Blender

Spoon

Measuring Spoons

A Glass Bowl
