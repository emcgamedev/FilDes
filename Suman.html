Suman

A traditional Filipino delicacy made with glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk and sugar, wrapped in banana leaves. There are many variations of suman, differing in fillings and cooking methods.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

0 min.

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1

Wash and soak: Wash the glutinous rice and soak it in water for at least 4 hours.

step 2

Prepare the coconut milk mixture: In a pot, combine coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Heat over medium heat until the sugar dissolves.

step 3

Drain and add rice: Drain the soaked rice and add it to the hot coconut milk mixture. Stir well and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes, or until the rice is almost cooked through.

step 4

Wrapping: Prepare banana leaves by cutting them into squares. Lay a banana leaf square on a flat surface. Place a spoonful of the cooked rice mixture in the center. Fold the banana leaf to enclose the rice,

step 5

forming a pouch. Secure the pouch with a toothpick (optional). Repeat with the remaining rice mixture and banana leaves.

step 6

Steaming: Arrange the wrapped suman in a steamer basket. Steam for 30-40 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and translucent.

step 7

Serving: Let the suman cool slightly before unwrapping. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Ingredients

Glutinous rice (2 cups)

Coconut milk (2 cups)

Sugar (1/2 cup)

Salt (1/4 tsp)

Banana leaves (for wrapping)

Tools

Large Bowl

steamer

whisk

spoon

banana leaves

toothpick
